Six Sigma

What is Six Sigma?

Six Sigma is a data-driven methodology for process improvement and quality management, aiming to reduce defects and variability in business processes. Developed by Motorola in the 1980s, it uses statistical tools and techniques to identify and eliminate root causes of errors, targeting near-perfection (3.4 defects per million opportunities). It emphasizes DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) for problem-solving and relies on trained professionals (e.g., Green Belts, Black Belts) to implement projects. Widely used across industries, Six Sigma enhances efficiency, reduces costs, and improves customer satisfaction.

What's the point of Six Sigma?

  • Eliminate waste & excess cost.
  • Reduce variation & defects.
  • Improve process & customer experience.

    DMAIC Methodology

    Six Sigma is grounded in the DMAIC methodology. The name 'DMAIC' is an acronym for the five phases which comprise the methodology. I'll go through each briefly.

    1. Define

    Set project goals and boundaries in alignment with your organizations:

    • Business Goals.
    • Customer Needs.
    • The process that requires improvement.

    2. Measure

    Pinpoint the location/ source of problems by building a factual understanding of the existing process conditions. Also, establish a baseline capability level

    3. Analyze

    4. Improve

    5. Control
